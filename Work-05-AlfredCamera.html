AlfredCamera

Retail payment app

Background

Alfred Camera is committed to helping users solve home security issues. The Alfred Camera App allows users to transform their spare smartphones into surveillance cameras for free. Additionally, Alfred Camera is a subscription-based app, and users need to upgrade to unlock advanced features. This project aims to optimize the subscription page starting from known issues to improve the page conversion rate.

Designer

1 designer

Role

I was responsible for UX design and UI design, collaborating closely with the Growth, Data, Development, and QA Teams.

Process

Proposal

I am honored to say that I am the initiator of the project. Based on data observations, we have noticed a gradual decline in our North Star metric, active subscribers. Therefore, I have identified the current known issues within the user journey of subscriptions and proposed optimization directions to the Growth team.



Goal and Observation

After discussing with the Growth and Data Team, we discovered that the conversion rate at the subscription page within the funnel has declined more than in other stages. Therefore, we have decided to prioritize the optimization of the subscription page along the entire subscription path, starting with the known issues. Our goal is to increase the conversion rate by 2%.


Design hypothesis

Firstly, I collected insights from the surveys sent to users regularly and the reports from the data team. Based on these insights, we identified the problems we need to address in this iteration. After discussing with the team, we generated several possible design hypotheses for each problem.


Design solutions

From the defined design hypotheses mentioned above, I brainstormed various versions of designs and discussed them with the team.



Based on data insights, with 51.76% of users subscribing to Premium due to specific popular features, we have selected the #02 - Popular Feature Spotlight to effectively communicate the value of these features. In line with our hypotheses for this experiment, we have initiated the validation process with minimal changes as the first step.



I explored various visual solutions during the UI phase, such as using photos, icons, and abstract illustrations. After discussions with the team, we concluded that photos would be the most effective choice. We believed that photos would enable users to better visualize and relate to different usage scenarios



A/B testing

In the A/B testing plan, we have set up three experimental groups, each emphasizing different popular features while using the existing subscription page as the control group. Additionally, we have selected countries with a significant decline in subscription rates as the target areas for this experiment.


Handoff

In addition to delivering UX flows and UI mockups, I also defined detailed specifications to assist the development team in building an interface that supports multiple languages and adapts to different devices.


Learning

After releasing and observing the results, the conversion rate of the experimental group was indeed higher than the control group, but the results did not rise to our expected goal. Even so, we gained other learnings, which helped us continue with the next iteration. For example: through data observation, we found that many users who give up upgrading will click on the card (popular feature) to get more information. We reasonably speculate that users are indeed interested in popular features and want to get more information about paid features.
