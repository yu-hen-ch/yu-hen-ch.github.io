PXPay

Retail payment app

Background

PXMart, established in Taiwan in October 1998, stands as the largest retail company across Taiwan, boasting the highest number of branches among all supermarkets in the region. In 2019, PXMart introduced PXPay, a mobile payment solution for use within its stores. With its substantial business presence, PXPay has amassed around 4 million Monthly Active Users (MAU), primarily targeting female office workers aged 45-55.

In May 2021, the sudden outbreak of Covid-19 resulted in a widespread economic downturn and weakened industries across Taiwan. Responding to this challenge, the Taiwanese government initiated the "Quintuple Stimulus Voucher" policy. Under this policy, the government directly distributed vouchers worth 5,000 NT dollars each, with the aim of boosting consumer spending and revitalizing the economy. Consequently, this project was thoughtfully designed to enable users to utilize the "Quintuple Stimulus Vouchers" at PXMart through pxpay, resulting in a meticulously planned project.
