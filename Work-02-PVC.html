Formosa Plastic

Orders management plateform

Overview

Formosa Plastic is a raw material company. This system is developed for users to manage their quotations and orders. On the other flip, reducing the burden on salespeople in Formosa Plastic and helping them digitize original paper processes about trading are also important. Thus, we have to analyze the needs of users(Formosa Plastic’s clients) and salespeople for designing the client-side and sales-side of this system.

Team

Designer team of 4

Role

In this project, I worked at the agency hired by Formosa Plastic, including, PM, SA, Design, Dev, QA Team

Duration

8 Months

Full Project Scope

I was responsible for Shipment, Notification, and Userguide in the Client-side of the Formosa Plastic Order System.


Challenges

Industry knowledge with a large amount of information

We have to understand all the domain knowledge through many meetings, including international trading habits, rules about how orders merged or split, and the relationship between factories and ports.

No chance to get in touch with end-users

Because of trade secrets, Formosa Plastic was unwilling to provide us with its customer list. Therefore, we have no choice but to understand users(Formosa Plastic’s clients) by interviewing employees and stakeholders in Formosa Plastic.

Multiple stakeholders from different departments

In terms of the process of international trade in Formosa Plastic, different departments are responsible for different work. We conducted several interviews with employees in different departments to understand their collaboration and processes.!

Restrictions from the original ERP system

To concatenate their ERP system, we interview employees from Information Department for clarifying the situation. Then we considered the restrictions of the ERP system and provide feasible solutions.

Design Quickview

Shipment

Users can see their shipping orders in different statuses on the shipment page. There are tabs and filters that help users find the order they want quickly.

Shipping Form(Edit mode)

By using multi-step forms, users could easier complete the complicated form. Also, there is a Live chat fixed on the right side of the window to support users in solving their problems.

Form(View mode)

In the view mode, users would see the explanation of the current status in the first part, if something unusual happened with the shipping order.

Userguide

Once new users get into the system, the pop-up modal helps them get a better sense of where the key controls are at.

Process

Stakeholder Interview

My team met with Formosa Plastic’s stakeholders for a series of QA sessions to learn about their needs, processes, and industry knowledge.



After the interviews, we tried to identify all the processes , and we create flow charts with design workshops.


Users

User profiles which used the insights gathered by interviewing the project owner and stakeholders, help I understand users’ goals and frustrations in the original paper processes.


Journey Mapping

Based on interviews and discussions, developing the future-state journey map helps my team members not only empathize with users but also envision opportunities for design. Also, the process of creating a map forces our conversation with clients. Thus, we could be aligned and conveyed our thought easily.



After analyzing the journey map, I set up design goals:

Design Goals

01 - Help users successfully fill out the complicated form
02 - Help users find the shipping order they need
03 - Keep users informed of abnormal-state shipping orders
04 - Notify users for every change of shipping orders

Design solutions

01 - Architecture : Not in the form of Process-per-tab ⇀
02 - Table : Prioritize tags in Preparation’s lists ⇀
03 - Table : Use Pagination not Infinite Scrolling ⇀
04 - Form：Increase success rate and decrease completion time ⇀

01 - Architecture : Not in the form of Process-per-tab

During desk research, I discover that most Order Page of EC websites is in the form of Process-per-tab. But our Shipment page doesn’t follow that because shipping orders in Formosa Plastic would change the status back and forth between Stand By and In Progress.

If it was adopted Process-per-tab, users might often switch tabs to find the shipping order. So Stand By, In Progress, and On-boarding are included in the “Preparation Tab”.

02 - Table : Prioritize tags in Preparation’s lists

According to different processes and statuses of shipping orders in Preparation’s lists, I categorized three types of tags as follows:

  • Alert tag

    • Focus on the table, the priority of orders with error status is the highest. To keep users informed of abnormal-state shipping orders, I choose red as the color of tags and pin those orders on top.

  • Process tag

    • To help users recognize orders in different processes quickly, and consider a precondition that priority of normal-state tags couldn't higher than those abnormal-state. Thus, I design dots with the same color on tags and filters.

  • Tooltip

    • When users notice the abnormal-state orders, we hope they can find out the explanation without clicking to enter the order page. In addition, considering table width causes that word limit, I use tooltips to achieve our goals.

    03 - Table : Use Pagination not Infinite Scrolling

    “Which method should be chosen for the project, Pagination or Infinite Scrolling? “It's a frequently encountered problem and there are many articles that have discussed it. Each method has its strengths and weaknesses, then I analyze them and consider the situation of this system. Eventually, I selected the Pagination for the following reasons:

  • Content is text not Image

    • Reading text takes much longer than Images, so users need time to absorb information, they can control when to continue to the next page .


  • Look for the needed shipping order quickly

    • Having a paginated interface lets the user keep an about location of the shipping order.

    04 - Form：Increase success rate and decrease completion time

    Because of the need for Formosa Plastic's international shipping process, users have to fill out multiple fields and upload many documents. To help them have a high success rate of application, I designed this function in the following ways:

  • Multi-step form & Groups

    • Since breaking complex tasks into smaller steps can decrease cognitive load, the form is split into 4 steps and each is divided into groups.

  • Suitable form width

    • Considering line length affects the legibility of text, I set the suitable form width.

  • Live chat

    • There is a Live chat fixed on the right side of the window to support users in solving their problems.

    Design System Building

    We established styleguide, components, and a set of guidelines for usage. This not only improved efficiency during the product development but also ensured iteration of Formosa Plastic Order System in future could keep with design consistency easily.


    According to the product keywords: efficiency, neat, steady, I set the relevant colors. The font selection focuses on improving the recognition of numbers, so Roboto is used for English, and Source Han Sans is used for Chinese.
