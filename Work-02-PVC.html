Formosa Plastic

Orders management plateform

Background

Formosa Plastic is a raw material company. The system is developed to assist users in managing their quotations and orders. Additionally, it aims to alleviate the burden on Formosa Plastic's salespeople and facilitate the digitization of their traditional paper-based trading processes. Therefore, in designing both the client-side and sales-side of this system, it is essential to thoroughly analyze the needs of Formosa Plastic's clients and salespeople.

Design Team

Team of 4

Role

In this project, I worked at the agency hired by Formosa Plastic, including, PM, SA, Design, Dev, QA Team

Duration

8 Months

Full Project Scope

I was responsible for Shipment, Notification, and Userguide in the Client-side of the Formosa Plastic Order System.


Challenges

Industry knowledge with a large amount of information

We have to understand all the domain knowledge through many meetings, including international trading habits, rules about how orders merged or split, and the relationship between factories and ports.

No direct access to end-users

Because of trade secrets, Formosa Plastic was unwilling to provide us with its customer list. Therefore, we have no choice but to understand users(Formosa Plastic’s clients) by interviewing employees and stakeholders in Formosa Plastic.

Involvement of multiple stakeholders from different departments

In the international trade process at Formosa Plastic, different departments handle different responsibilities. We conducted several interviews with employees from various departments to understand their collaboration and processes.

ERP system limitations

To integrate with their ERP system, we interviewed employees from the Information Department to clarify the situation. Subsequently, we considered the restrictions of the ERP system and provided feasible solutions.

Design Quickview

Shipment

Users can see their shipping orders in different statuses on the shipment page. There are tabs and filters that help users find the order they want quickly.

Shipping Form(Edit mode)

By using multi-step forms, users could easier complete the complicated form. Also, there is a Live chat fixed on the right side of the window to support users in solving their problems.

Form(View mode)

In the view mode, users would see the explanation of the current status in the first part, if something unusual happened with the shipping order.

Once new users get into the system, the pop-up modal helps them get a better sense of where the key controls are at.

Process

Stakeholder Interview

My team met with Formosa Plastic’s stakeholders for a series of QA sessions to learn about their needs, processes, and industry knowledge.



After conducting the interviews, we proceeded to identify all the processes and created flow charts during design workshops.


Users

User profiles, derived from insights gathered through interviews with the project owner and stakeholders, helped us understand users' needs and pains with the original paper processes.


Journey Mapping

Based on interviews and discussions, creating a future-state journey map helps our team members understand the users' perspectives and identify design opportunities. This process also facilitates our communication with clients, ensuring alignment and clarity in our thoughts.



Following the analysis of the journey map, I established the following design goals:

Design Goals

01 - Assist users in successfully completing complex forms
02 - Facilitate users in locating required shipping orders
03 - Ensure users are promptly informed of shipping orders in abnormal states
04 - Provide users with notifications for any changes to their shipping orders

Design solutions

01 - Architecture : Not in the form of Process-per-tab ⇀
02 - Table : Prioritize tags in Preparation’s lists ⇀
03 - Table : Use Pagination not Infinite Scrolling ⇀
04 - Form：Increase success rate and decrease completion time ⇀

01 - Architecture: Not Following the Process-per-Tab Format

In my desk research, I discovered that most EC websites have an Order Page organized as Process-per-Tab. However, our Shipment page differs because Formosa Plastic's shipping orders frequently switch statuses between Stand By and In Progress.

Had we adopted the Process-per-Tab layout, users might have to frequently switch tabs to locate their shipping orders. Hence, we've included Stand By, In Progress, and On-boarding statuses under the “Preparation Tab”.

02 - Table: Prioritizing Tags in the Preparation Lists

Considering the diverse processes and statuses of shipping orders in the Preparation Lists, I've categorized three types of tags as follows:

  • Alert Tags

    • These tags are prominently displayed for orders with error statuses, ensuring users notice high-priority orders. To alert users about abnormal-state shipping orders, I've selected red as the tag color and pinned these orders at the top of the list.

  • Process Tags

    • To recognize orders in different stages quickly, I've incorporated dots with consistent colors on tags and filters. Notably, I've maintained a hierarchy where normal-state tags are not more prominent than abnormal-state tags.

  • Tooltips

    • To enhance usability and avoid word limit issues within the table width, tooltips are employed. Users can access explanations for abnormal-state orders without the need to click and enter the order page.

    03 - Table : Use Pagination not Infinite Scrolling

    The choice between Pagination and Infinite Scrolling is a common discussion in projects, and numerous articles have dissected this topic. Each method has its advantages and limitations. After a careful analysis, considering the specifics of this system, I've settled on Pagination for the following reasons:

  • Textual Content

    • As reading textual content demands more time than processing images, users require ample time to assimilate information. With Pagination, they can conveniently control when they proceed to the next page.


  • Efficient Searching

    • A paginated interface enables users to maintain a sense of the order's location, aiding them in quickly locating the desired shipping order.

    04 - Form: Enhancing Success Rate and Efficiency

    Cause the complicate of Formosa Plastic's international shipping process, users must complete numerous fields and upload several documents. To elevate their application success rate , I've devised the following strategies:

  • Multi-step Form & Grouping

    • Breaking down complex tasks into manageable steps minimizes cognitive load. The form has been segmented into four distinct steps, with each step further organized into groups.

  • Optimal Form Width

    • I've taken into account the impact of line length on text legibility and established an appropriate form width for optimal user experience.

  • Integrated Live Chat

    • To provide immediate assistance in addressing user queries, a fixed Live Chat feature is positioned on the window's right side. This real-time support aims to facilitate issue resolution during form completion.

    Building the Design System

    We established styleguide, components, and a set of guidelines for usage. This not only improved efficiency during the product development but also ensured iteration of Formosa Plastic Order System in future could keep with design consistency easily.


    According to the product keywords: efficiency, neat, steady, I set the relevant colors. The font selection focuses on improving the recognition of numbers, so Roboto is used for English, and Source Han Sans is used for Chinese.
